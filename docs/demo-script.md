## Recording Tips

**Setup:**
- Record screen with OBS Studio or QuickTime Player (Mac). OBS preferred for scene transitions.
- Resolution: 1920x1080 minimum. Record at 60fps for smooth scrolling.
- Use a good external mic or AirPods Pro at minimum. Record in a quiet room.
- Close all notifications, hide the dock, use a clean desktop.

**Performance:**
- Practice the full script at least 3 times before recording. Time yourself.
- Keep mouse movements smooth and deliberate -- no jittery cursor.
- Pause the mouse on each element for 1-2 seconds before moving to the next. Let the viewer's eye settle.
- Click with purpose. No double-clicks, no missed targets.

**Demo Flow:**
- Use mock mode for predictable, repeatable data. The dealData.ts fixtures ensure consistent demo content.
- Pre-fill the buyer context form before recording if needed -- or show the typing for authenticity.
- For the live session phase, use the scripted events from dealData.ts (03:12, 14:48, 28:06, 36:44, 47:31) to walk through each moment.

**Post-Production:**
- Add subtle background music: royalty-free, instrumental, low volume (10-15% of voiceover level). Search "tech product launch music" on Artlist or Epidemic Sound.
- Add gentle fade transitions between phases (0.5s crossfade, not hard cuts).
- Consider adding a thin lower-third bar during the architecture section with key specs: "Gemini 2.5 Flash | 10 Function Tools | WebSocket Streaming | Cloud Run"
- Export as MP4, H.264, at least 8 Mbps bitrate for crisp text rendering.

**Voiceover Style:**
- Speak at a confident, measured pace. Not rushed, not slow. Think podcast host, not auctioneer.
- Let key numbers land: "$84K", "seventy-two percent close rate", "twenty-two points". Pause slightly before and after.
- No filler words. If you stumble, stop and re-record that section.
- Vary energy: calmer during the hook, building through pre-call, peak energy during the live session, authoritative during architecture, quiet conviction on the close.
